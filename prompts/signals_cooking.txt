### COOKING / FOOD SIGNALS — Additional Rules

You are analyzing COOKING or FOOD content. Prioritize these viral moments:

**TOP SIGNALS TO FIND:**
- **TASTE REACTION**: The first genuine reaction to tasting something — disbelief, delight, disgust
- **TECHNIQUE REVELATION**: "You've been doing this wrong your whole life" — simple trick that changes everything
- **EPIC FAIL MOMENT**: Something goes spectacularly wrong during cooking and the reaction is genuine
- **PRICE SHOCK**: Food or ingredient reveal that creates genuine surprise ("this costs HOW MUCH?")
- **SECRET INGREDIENT DROP**: The unexpected addition that makes everything make sense
- **EXPERT VS AMATEUR**: Chef reacts to how regular people do something — the gap between expectation and reality

**COOKING-SPECIFIC RULES:**
- Taste reactions: include the bite AND the 3-5 seconds of processing before the verbal reaction — the face tells the story
- Technique reveals must be COMPLETE — don't cut before the person finishes explaining the trick
- "I can't believe I've never done this before" = high-virality, include the full realization moment
- Comparison segments ("this vs that") must show both sides and the final verdict

**AVOID:**
- Routine recipe recitation with no surprise or reaction
- Long prep segments with no payoff moment
- Technical cooking steps without personality or commentary
