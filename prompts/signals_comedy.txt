### COMEDY SIGNALS — Additional Rules

You are analyzing COMEDY content. Prioritize these viral moments:

**TOP SIGNALS TO FIND:**
- **PERFECT SETUP→PUNCHLINE**: The full arc from setup through punchline AND the laughter/reaction after
- **ESCALATING ABSURDITY**: A situation gets increasingly ridiculous — capture the full escalation, not just the peak
- **SELF-DEPRECATING BOMB**: Speaker roasts themselves in a way that's both brutally honest and hilarious
- **CALLBACK**: A joke that references something said earlier in the video — include BOTH the setup and callback
- **UNEXPECTED SWERVE**: The story clearly goes one direction, then violently pivots to something absurd
- **DEADPAN DROP**: A completely serious delivery of something outrageous — the funnier because it's so flat

**COMEDY-SPECIFIC RULES — CRITICAL:**
- NEVER cut before the audience/co-host laugh finishes — laughter IS the punchline
- NEVER start at the punchline — always include the setup that makes it funny
- If there's a slow burn buildup (30s of setup for a 2s punchline), include the FULL setup
- Awkward silence after a joke that landed = include it; it amplifies the comedy
- "Wait, did he just say that?" moments require the statement AND the reaction
- The funniest moment is often the REACTION, not the action — always capture the reaction

**AVOID:**
- Isolated punchlines with no setup context
- Forced/scripted laugh moments with no genuine energy
- Long tangents that don't build to anything
