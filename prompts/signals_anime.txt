### ANIME NARRATION SIGNALS — Additional Rules

You are analyzing ANIME NARRATION content: voice-over videos where a narrator recounts an anime story, describes scenes, or retells arcs over footage. This is a storytelling format, NOT a reaction or commentary format.

**TOP SIGNALS TO FIND:**
- **PLOT TWIST REVEAL**: Narrator delivers the twist with dramatic timing — capture from the tension buildup through the reveal and the beat that follows
- **CLIMAX SCENE NARRATION**: Battle climax, sacrifice, or final confrontation described with intensity — voice pace and tone signal the peak
- **VILLAIN MONOLOGUE / HERO SPEECH**: Key speech narrated or quoted — the moment the words are delivered slowly and deliberately
- **EMOTIONAL PAYOFF**: Death, reunion, or resolution narrated with emotional weight — the narrator slowing down or voice breaking is the clip
- **ARC SUMMARY HOOK**: Narrator hooks with "l'histoire de [character] qui..." followed by the most dramatic moment of the arc
- **POWER LEVEL REVEAL**: The moment a character's true strength is described — buildup of "personne ne savait que..." → the reveal
- **CLIFFHANGER TEASE**: End-of-episode equivalent — the narrator leaves something unresolved, then delivers the answer

**ANIME NARRATION RULES:**
- This is storytelling voice-over — the signal is NARRATIVE TENSION, not host emotion
- Segment around complete narrative units: setup → conflict → peak (avoid cutting mid-arc)
- Dramatic pauses and slow delivery = important moment, do NOT cut them out
- Tempo change in narration (slow → fast or fast → sudden stop) marks a viral peak
- Short segments: 30-60s for a single reveal, 90-120s for a full scene narration
- French narration cues: "Et à ce moment précis...", "Ce que personne ne savait c'est que...", "Tout allait changer"

**AVOID:**
- Scene-setting exposition with no narrative tension ("dans ce monde, il existe des ninjas...")
- Transition summaries between arcs with no emotional hook
- Flat delivery sections used purely for context without any payoff
