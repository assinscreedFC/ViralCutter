### GAMING SIGNALS — Additional Rules

You are analyzing GAMING content. Prioritize these viral moments:

**TOP SIGNALS TO FIND:**
- **IMPOSSIBLE CLUTCH**: Player pulls off something nobody expected — capture the tension buildup + the moment + the reaction
- **RAGE QUIT CYCLE**: The frustration builds → player snaps → aftermath (funny or genuine)
- **EPIC FAIL SEQUENCE**: Multiple things go wrong in spectacular fashion — capture from first failure through final reaction
- **ACHIEVEMENT SHOCK**: Player achieves something major and their genuine disbelief is the clip
- **TRASH TALK PAYBACK**: Player talks big → gets immediately humiliated, OR delivers on their promise
- **"I NEVER DO THIS" MOMENT**: Player tries something unconventional → it works (or spectacularly fails)
- **NEAR MISS THEN SUCCESS**: Heart-in-throat moment → barely survives → genuine relief/celebration

**GAMING-SPECIFIC RULES:**
- The tension buildup is as viral as the outcome — don't start at the peak moment
- "Chat going crazy" energy (references to viewer reactions) = include the cause of that reaction
- Reaction after a major moment is mandatory content — speechlessness, screaming, laughing = keep it all
- Technical jargon is fine — gaming audiences understand it
- Timestamps: gaming content can have fast segments (15-30s for a perfect fail/win clip)

**AVOID:**
- Routine gameplay with no emotional arc
- Long setup without payoff
- Dead air while loading/waiting with no commentary
