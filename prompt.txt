### VIRAL SIGNAL TAXONOMY — Hunt for these signals:
- **SHOCK/SURPRISE**       : unexpected revelation, twist, confession, betrayal
- **CONFLICT/TENSION**     : heated disagreement, confrontation, direct challenge, debate turning point
- **RELATABILITY**         : universal experience told in a way nobody has heard before
- **COUNTER-INTUITIVE**    : info that destroys a common belief ("Actually, the opposite is true")
- **RAW EMOTION**          : real anger, real joy, real fear — unscripted and unfiltered
- **SOCIAL PROOF**         : "everyone does X" or "I asked 1000 people and..."
- **STAKES**               : something important is on the line — money, reputation, relationship, health

### STRICT VIRAL RULES (The "ViralCutter Standard"):

**1. NO GARBAGE STARTS**
- NEVER start with filler words (Um, Uh, So, And then, Well, Right, Basically...)
- NEVER start with low-energy openers ("Hi guys, today we...", "Welcome back to...")
- ALWAYS start with a Hook: strong statement, a question, or an action verb
- *Bad:* "(10s) So, I was thinking..." → *Good:* "(12s) I almost died yesterday."

**2. THE STANDALONE TEST**
- Could a complete stranger watch this with ZERO prior context and understand it?
- If it contains "that's why he said that" (and "he" is unknown) → FAIL. Expand the start.
- Capture the ENTIRE story from its natural beginning to its absolute end.
- NEVER cut a story short to fit a time limit. If the full story needs 1m20s, extract 1m20s.

**3. NARRATIVE ARC & PERFECT ENDING**
- **Hook (0–5s):** The premise — what's at stake, what's the tension?
- **Middle:** Tension, buildup, value delivery, escalation
- **End:** Punchline, resolution, reaction, payoff — 100% finished and satisfying
- NEVER cut mid-sentence. NEVER cut right after a punchline — let the reaction breathe.

**4. DURATION — Variable, Story-Driven**
- Each segment MUST be between {min_duration}s and {max_duration}s.
- MANDATORY SELF-CHECK: before writing each segment, compute end_time_ref − start_time_ref.
  → If result < {min_duration}: STOP. DISCARD these timestamps. Find a wider scene. DO NOT output this segment.
  → If result > {max_duration}: STOP. DISCARD these timestamps. Split into parts instead. DO NOT output this segment.
  → ONLY write the segment if the check PASSES. No exceptions.
- {min_duration}s is the ABSOLUTE MINIMUM (hard floor), NOT a target. Do NOT stop at {min_duration}s just because the constraint is satisfied.
- {max_duration}s is the ABSOLUTE MAXIMUM. If a story exceeds {max_duration}s → SPLIT it into parts (see Rule 5). Do NOT exceed {max_duration}s in a single segment.
- TARGET: 60-80s. If the story is still building at {min_duration}s, KEEP GOING until the punchline/reaction/payoff lands.
- A segment that ends at 75s with a complete arc is BETTER than one cut at {min_duration}s without payoff.
- WRONG: start_time_ref=500, end_time_ref=513 (13s — violates minimum → DISCARD, find new timestamps)
- WRONG: start_time_ref=500, end_time_ref=543 (43s — story likely unfinished → extend)
- WRONG: start_time_ref=500, end_time_ref=662 (162s — exceeds maximum → SPLIT into parts)
- PREFERRED: start_time_ref=500, end_time_ref=575 (75s — complete arc with reaction)

**TIMESTAMP-TEXT CONSISTENCY (critical)**
- start_text MUST be words that appear in the transcript at or just before start_time_ref.
- end_text MUST be words that appear in the transcript at or just before end_time_ref.
- NEVER write start_text or end_text from a completely different timestamp than the one you declared.
- BAD: start_time_ref=120, end_time_ref=195 but end_text is a phrase you saw at 1585s → FORBIDDEN
- GOOD: start_time_ref=1542, end_time_ref=1615 → start_text and end_text both come from the 1542–1615s region

**5. SPEAKER NAMES — Only Direct Addresses**
- A name is valid ONLY if it is used to directly address or identify a participant (e.g., "Arrête ça, Maxime !", "Gotaga a dit que...").
- NEVER use a name that appears as a reference, comparison, or metaphor ("tu ressembles à Gérard", "c'est un vrai Louis de Funès", "joue comme Messi") — that name belongs to someone OUTSIDE the conversation, not a speaker.
- If a participant's real name is unclear: describe them by their role or action ("le présentateur", "le joueur", "celui qui avoue").
- BAD: someone says "tu ressembles à Gérard" → title uses "Gérard" as a speaker name → WRONG
- GOOD: someone says "Arrête, Djilsi !" → "Djilsi" is a real participant and can be used

**6. MULTI-PART SYSTEM**
Split a segment into N consecutive parts when:
  (1) The complete story naturally EXCEEDS {max_duration}s → you MUST split
  (2) A cliffhanger creates suspense and boosts retention → you MAY split
  (3) N shorter punchy parts beat 1 long flat segment → you MAY split

Rules for N-part segments (N can be 2, 3, 4 or more — no limit):
- Title format: "Base Title — Partie 1/N", "Base Title — Partie 2/N", ..., "Base Title — Partie N/N"
- Each part MUST be between {min_duration}s and {max_duration}s
- Parts MUST be consecutive: end of part K = start of part K+1
- Set "part_number": K and "total_parts": N in each JSON entry
- For standalone segments: "part_number": 1, "total_parts": 1
- Example: 3-minute story with max=60s → 3 parts of ~60s each (part_number 1, 2, 3 — total_parts 3)
